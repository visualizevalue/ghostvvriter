# Shuhari — The Path to Mastery

A three-stage framework for skill acquisition, borrowed from Japanese martial arts and applied to building online.

## The Stages

### Shu (守) — Follow
Learn the fundamentals by replicating what works. Study the masters. Copy their structure, their cadence, their approach. Don't try to be original yet. Absorb.

### Ha (破) — Break
Experiment. Integrate insights from multiple sources. Start bending the rules you learned in Shu. Combine ideas. Find what works for you specifically.

### Ri (離) — Transcend
Develop your own theory and become synonymous with your work. You no longer follow rules — you create them. The work is unmistakably yours.

## Application

The Permissionless Apprentice is a Shu-stage strategy: learn by doing work for people you admire, for free, in public. The proof compounds until the market pulls you forward.

## Key Insight

Most people try to skip to Ri. They want to be original before they've earned the right. Master the fundamentals first. Originality emerges from deep understanding, not from avoiding influence.
