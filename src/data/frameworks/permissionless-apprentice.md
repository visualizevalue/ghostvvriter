# The Permissionless Apprentice

A strategy for building traction online without credentials, connections, or capital.

## Core Idea

Do work for people you admire — without asking permission. Share it publicly. The work is the proof. The proof creates the opportunity.

## The Process

1. **Find someone whose work you admire** — study what they do, how they communicate, what they sell.
2. **Do work that helps them** — redesign their landing page, write a case study, build a tool, visualize their ideas.
3. **Share it publicly** — post the work, tag them, explain your thinking.
4. **Repeat** — whether they respond or not, you win. The work builds your portfolio, your skills, and your reputation.

## Key Frameworks

### Positive-Sum Learning
Your permissionless projects allow you to learn while providing value. Even if no one responds, you're still winning. The learning is the floor. The opportunity is the ceiling.

### Portfolios over Posturing
Show, don't tell. A portfolio of real work beats a resume of credentials. The market rewards demonstrated ability, not claimed ability.

### Frictionless Value
Make it easy for people to say yes. Don't ask for a meeting. Don't send a pitch deck. Send the finished work.

### Building in Public
Document the process. Share the journey. The byproduct of your work (the "sawdust") builds an audience that compounds over time.

## Key Insight

"No one cares what you can do, everyone cares what you can do for them."

The Permissionless Apprentice inverts the traditional job search. Instead of asking for permission to prove yourself, you prove yourself first. The permission follows.
