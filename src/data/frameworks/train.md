# TRAIN — The Five Elements of Visual Communication

A framework for creating visuals that communicate ideas clearly, even without a design background.

## The Elements

### T — Typography
Every typographic decision is saying something. Font choice, weight, size, spacing — each one carries meaning. Choose deliberately.

### R — Restraint
Make one decision to save a thousand decisions. Constrain your palette, your fonts, your layout. Restraint forces better ideas. If you can't hide behind constantly tweaking color, typography, and images, you're forced to come up with better ideas.

### A — Alignment
Good design is determined by invisible relationships. Elements that are aligned feel intentional. Misalignment feels accidental. The grid is your friend.

### I — Image Treatment
Consistent treatment creates consistent brand. Apply the same filters, crops, and color treatments across all visuals. Recognition compounds.

### N — Negative Space
Space is not there to fill. It's there to frame. What you leave out is as important as what you include. White space gives ideas room to breathe.

## Key Insight

You don't need to be a designer to communicate visually. You need constraints. TRAIN provides five — follow them and the visuals take care of themselves.
